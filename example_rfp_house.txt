REQUEST FOR PROPOSAL (RFP)
Construction of a Single-Family Residential Home
Reference: RFP-2026-RESIDENTIAL-0042
Issue Date: March 17, 2026
Submission Deadline: April 30, 2026

════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
SECTION 1 — PROJECT OVERVIEW
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

1.1 Issuing Party
The Beaumont Family ("Client") hereby issues this Request for Proposal for the
design and construction of a custom single-family residence located at:

    Lot 14, Maplewood Estates
    4820 Ridgecrest Drive
    Austin, Texas 78735

The selected general contractor will be responsible for the complete construction
of the home in accordance with the specifications outlined in this document,
applicable local building codes, and the State of Texas residential construction
standards.

1.2 Project Summary
The Client requires a modern farmhouse-style single-family home of approximately
3,200 square feet of conditioned living space, with an attached three-car garage.
The home must be designed to accommodate a family of five, including aging-in-place
features for one elderly parent. Construction must be completed within 14 months
of contract execution.

════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
SECTION 2 — SITE & LOT REQUIREMENTS
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

2.1 Lot Characteristics
The lot measures 0.75 acres (32,670 sq ft) and is predominantly flat with a
gentle 2% grade sloping toward the rear. Contractors must perform a geotechnical
survey prior to foundation work and submit results to the Client within 30 days
of contract signing.

2.2 Setbacks and Zoning
All structures must comply with Maplewood Estates HOA setback requirements:
- Front setback: minimum 30 feet from the curb
- Rear setback: minimum 25 feet from the rear property line
- Side setbacks: minimum 10 feet on each side
- Maximum lot coverage: 40% of total lot area
- Maximum building height: 35 feet above grade

2.3 Site Preparation
The contractor shall be responsible for:
- Clearing and grubbing the lot of all existing vegetation within the building envelope
- Grading and compaction per geotechnical recommendations
- Installation of erosion control measures during construction
- Connection to municipal water, sewer, and electrical utilities at the street

════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
SECTION 3 — ARCHITECTURAL & STRUCTURAL REQUIREMENTS
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

3.1 Architectural Style
The home shall follow a modern farmhouse aesthetic featuring:
- Board-and-batten siding in white or off-white (Benjamin Moore White Dove or equivalent)
- Black aluminum-clad windows and exterior doors
- Metal standing-seam roof in charcoal gray
- Covered front porch spanning the full width of the facade (minimum 8 feet deep)
- Covered rear patio of at least 400 square feet

3.2 Foundation
- Engineered post-tension slab-on-grade foundation
- Minimum slab thickness: 5 inches
- Foundation design must be stamped by a licensed structural engineer registered in Texas
- All foundation work must comply with IRC 2021 and local Austin amendments

3.3 Framing
- Exterior walls: 2x6 wood framing at 16 inches on center
- Interior load-bearing walls: 2x4 minimum, per structural drawings
- Roof framing: engineered roof trusses at 24 inches on center
- All lumber must meet or exceed #2 Southern Yellow Pine grading

3.4 Exterior Envelope
- Continuous house wrap (Tyvek HomeWrap or equivalent) under all siding
- Minimum R-20 cavity insulation in all exterior walls
- Minimum R-49 blown insulation in attic
- Minimum R-10 under-slab insulation at perimeter
- All windows and doors must be ENERGY STAR certified
- Windows: double-pane, low-E glass, U-factor ≤ 0.30, SHGC ≤ 0.25

════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
SECTION 4 — FLOOR PLAN REQUIREMENTS
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

4.1 General Layout
The home shall be a single-story design with the following program:

    Ground Floor (approx. 3,200 sq ft conditioned):
    - Open-concept great room (living + dining + kitchen): minimum 800 sq ft
    - Primary bedroom suite: minimum 400 sq ft including en-suite bathroom
    - Three additional bedrooms: minimum 150 sq ft each
    - Two full guest bathrooms
    - Dedicated home office: minimum 120 sq ft
    - Laundry room: minimum 80 sq ft with utility sink
    - Mud room / drop zone adjacent to garage entry
    - Walk-in pantry adjacent to kitchen: minimum 60 sq ft
    - Attached three-car garage: minimum 700 sq ft

4.2 Ceiling Heights
- Great room, kitchen, and foyer: minimum 12-foot ceilings
- All other habitable rooms: minimum 9-foot ceilings
- Garage: minimum 10-foot clear height to accommodate standard SUVs

4.3 Accessibility (Aging-in-Place)
One bedroom and one full bathroom must be fully ADA-accessible, including:
- Zero-threshold shower with fold-down bench and grab bars
- Wider doorways (minimum 36 inches clear) throughout the accessible wing
- Lever-style door hardware throughout the accessible wing
- Blocking installed in all bathroom walls for future grab bar installation
- Single-story design with no steps between the garage and the accessible suite

════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
SECTION 5 — MECHANICAL, ELECTRICAL & PLUMBING
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

5.1 HVAC
- Two-zone high-efficiency central HVAC system (one zone per wing)
- Minimum SEER2 rating: 18 for cooling, HSPF2 ≥ 9 for heating
- Zoned programmable smart thermostats (Ecobee or equivalent) in each zone
- Fresh air ventilation system (ERV) to meet ASHRAE 62.2 standards
- All ductwork located within conditioned space; no ducts in unconditioned attic
- Whole-house air filtration: MERV-13 minimum

5.2 Plumbing
- Tankless gas water heater with a minimum 199,000 BTU input and 0.95 EF rating
- Recirculating hot water loop to all bathrooms and kitchen
- Pre-plumb for water softener in utility room
- Outdoor hose bibs: minimum 4 locations (front, rear, each side)
- Irrigation stub-out at water meter for future landscape irrigation system
- All supply lines: PEX-A with Uponor or equivalent manifold system

5.3 Electrical
- 400-amp main electrical service panel
- Whole-house surge protection at the main panel
- EV charging rough-in in garage: two dedicated 50-amp, 240V circuits with conduit
- Solar-ready conduit from roof to electrical panel and utility room
- USB-A and USB-C outlets in every bedroom, kitchen, and office
- Smart lighting control wiring (Lutron Caseta compatible) throughout
- Dedicated 20-amp circuits for all kitchen appliances
- Whole-house generator transfer switch pre-wire (22kW minimum capacity)

5.4 Low Voltage & Technology
- Structured wiring panel in dedicated telecom closet
- Cat-6A Ethernet drops in every room (minimum 2 per room, 4 in office)
- Whole-home Wi-Fi access point rough-in (ceiling-mounted) in every room
- Pre-wire for 5.1 surround sound in great room and primary bedroom
- Video doorbell and smart lock pre-wire at front, rear, and garage doors
- Whole-house security system rough-in with contacts on all windows and doors
- Outdoor speakers pre-wire: front porch (2), rear patio (4)

════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
SECTION 6 — INTERIOR FINISHES
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

6.1 Flooring
- Great room, kitchen, hallways, office: 7.5-inch wide white oak engineered hardwood,
  minimum 3mm wear layer, minimum AC4 rating
- All bedrooms: same engineered hardwood (no carpet)
- All bathrooms and laundry: large-format porcelain tile, minimum 24"x24"
- Mud room: porcelain tile, slip-resistant rating R10 or better

6.2 Kitchen
- Custom or semi-custom cabinetry, full-overlay doors, soft-close hinges and drawer slides
- Quartz countertops, minimum 1.5-inch thickness
- Kitchen island: minimum 4 feet x 8 feet with seating for 4 on one side
- Commercial-style 48-inch range (gas or dual-fuel) rough-in
- Built-in refrigerator rough-in (36-inch cabinet depth)
- Dishwasher: panel-ready rough-in
- Farmhouse apron-front kitchen sink (36 inches minimum)
- Pot filler rough-in above range
- Under-cabinet lighting rough-in (LED)

6.3 Primary Bathroom
- Freestanding soaking tub
- Walk-in shower: minimum 5 feet x 4 feet with frameless glass enclosure
- Double vanity: minimum 72 inches wide
- Private water closet with door
- Heated tile floor (electric mat, client-supplied thermostat)
- Linen closet within or directly adjacent to bathroom

6.4 Interior Doors & Trim
- Interior doors: solid-core, 8-foot tall, flat panel, painted white
- All casing and base trim: 3.5-inch flat profile
- Crown molding in great room, foyer, and primary suite only
- Built-in bookshelves flanking fireplace in great room

6.5 Fireplace
- One gas fireplace in great room: minimum 48-inch linear burner
- Full-height shiplap surround with floating wood mantle
- Direct-vent gas with remote ignition

════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
SECTION 7 — EXTERIOR FEATURES
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

7.1 Landscaping (Hardscape Only — Softscape by Owner)
- Concrete driveway: minimum 18 feet wide, broom finish with expansion joints
- Concrete front walkway from driveway to front porch
- Rear patio: minimum 400 sq ft, stamped or pavers (owner to select pattern)
- Gravel or decomposed granite perimeter drainage swale along foundation

7.2 Garage
- Three-car garage with two separate garage door openings:
  one double-car bay (16 feet wide) and one single-car bay (9 feet wide)
- LiftMaster 8500W or equivalent wall-mount operators, Wi-Fi enabled
- Insulated garage doors, minimum R-12
- Epoxy-ready concrete floor (contractor to provide flat, smooth finish)
- Utility sink rough-in in garage

7.3 Outdoor Living
- Covered rear patio with ceiling fans (2 minimum) and outdoor-rated lighting
- Natural gas stub-out at rear patio for future outdoor kitchen
- 240V outlet on rear patio for future hot tub
- Exterior outlets: minimum GFCI outlet every 20 feet around perimeter

════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
SECTION 8 — SUSTAINABILITY & ENERGY REQUIREMENTS
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

8.1 Energy Performance
- The completed home must achieve a HERS Index Score of 50 or below
- A third-party HERS rater must be engaged by the contractor at contractor's expense
- Blower door test result: ≤ 2.0 ACH50

8.2 Water Conservation
- All toilets: maximum 1.28 GPF (WaterSense certified)
- All faucets: maximum 1.5 GPM (WaterSense certified)
- Shower heads: maximum 2.0 GPM

8.3 Materials
- All lumber from FSC-certified sources where available
- No VOC paints and finishes (VOC limit: ≤ 50 g/L for interior flat paint)
- Blown-in cellulose or fiberglass for attic insulation (no spray foam in attic)

════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
SECTION 9 — PROJECT SCHEDULE & CONTRACTUAL REQUIREMENTS
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

9.1 Schedule
- Geotechnical survey and foundation permit: within 30 days of contract execution
- Foundation complete: within 60 days of permit issuance
- Framing complete: within 120 days of contract execution
- Dry-in (roof and windows installed): within 150 days of contract execution
- Certificate of occupancy: within 14 months of contract execution

9.2 Warranty
- Contractor must provide a minimum 1-year workmanship warranty on all labor
- Structural warranty: 10 years per Texas Residential Construction Liability Act
- All manufacturer warranties must be transferred to the Client at closing

9.3 Insurance & Licensing
- Contractor must hold a valid Texas residential contractor registration
- General liability insurance: minimum $2,000,000 per occurrence
- Workers' compensation insurance required for all on-site personnel
- Proof of insurance must be provided before work commences

9.4 Payment Terms
- 10% deposit upon contract execution
- Progress draws tied to completion of defined milestones (schedule to be agreed)
- 5% retainage held until final punch list is cleared and CO is issued
- All subcontractors must be paid within 30 days of general contractor receiving draw

9.5 Permitting
- Contractor is responsible for obtaining all necessary permits including:
  building, electrical, plumbing, mechanical, and site permits
- All permit fees are the responsibility of the contractor and must be included in bid
- All inspections must be passed before proceeding to the next phase

SECTION 10 — PROPOSAL SUBMISSION REQUIREMENTS
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Proposals must include:
1. Fixed-price bid (no cost-plus bids will be accepted)
2. Detailed scope-of-work breakdown by trade
3. Proposed construction schedule with milestones
4. List of proposed subcontractors and their license numbers
5. Portfolio of at least three comparable completed projects
6. Proof of insurance and contractor registration
7. References from at least three recent clients

Submit proposals electronically to: rfp@beaumont-family.com
All questions must be submitted by April 15, 2026.
The Client reserves the right to reject any or all proposals.
